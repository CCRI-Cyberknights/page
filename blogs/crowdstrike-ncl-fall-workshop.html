CrowdStrike x NCL Fall Workshop: Building Careers and Connections in Cybersecurity

Key takeaways from the CrowdStrike x NCL Fall Workshop: internship opportunities, resume building tips, and how NCL competition experience translates to industry success.

October 22, 2025 by Zachary Hartmann Career

Recently, members of the CCRI Cyber Club had the opportunity to join the CrowdStrike x NCL Fall Workshop — and it was easily one of the most valuable events of the semester. The session, hosted by CrowdStrike university recruiter Melanie, brought together students from across the National Cyber League (NCL) community to talk resumes, interviews, and career growth in the cybersecurity industry.

From the start, the energy was great. Melanie and several CrowdStrike team members — representing incident response, threat hunting, intelligence, and red teaming — joined the call to give students hands-on career advice in specialized breakout rooms. It wasn't your typical "career talk" either; it was a deep dive into how to actually land cybersecurity roles and internships with real-world relevance.

Career Prep, the CrowdStrike Way

The workshop focused heavily on resume and interview preparation, which fits perfectly with the Cyber Club's mission to prepare students for their cybersecurity careers. Each breakout room was tailored to a specific career path, so whether you're into red teaming, forensics, or threat intel, you could get targeted advice directly from professionals in that field.

Melanie also broke down the CrowdStrike internship programs, including their 12-week summer internships and the Launch program designed for freshmen and sophomores. Both offer hands-on experience, mentorship, and a look into CrowdStrike's world of digital defense. She even mentioned how CrowdStrike's early interns are often trained with the goal of being hired full-time — something every student was excited to hear.

Why NCL Matters More Than Ever

One of the biggest takeaways was how much CrowdStrike values NCL participation. Since CrowdStrike uses Cyber Skyline — the same platform NCL runs on — students who compete in NCL already have a head start. Melanie encouraged everyone to list NCL experience on their resumes, emphasizing that employers notice when students are gaining real, hands-on practice through competitions.

That validation is huge for us at CCRI, since NCL is one of our club's cornerstone activities. It's motivating to know that the skills we sharpen through competitions are directly relevant to major cybersecurity employers.

NCL and Cyber Skyline Connection

The National Cyber League (NCL) uses the Cyber Skyline platform for their competitions, which is the same platform CrowdStrike uses for technical assessments. This means:

  • NCL participants are already familiar with the platform used in industry
  • Competition skills directly translate to job interview scenarios
  • Employers recognize NCL experience as valuable hands-on practice

Industry Insight and Inspiration

Beyond career prep, the workshop gave us a deeper look into CrowdStrike's company culture. We learned about the company's origins, its focus on intelligence-driven defense, and its global presence. The CrowdStrike team talked about their Falcon platform, their emphasis on research, and how they continue to innovate to stay ahead of modern threats.

The recruiters also shared practical insights about technical assessments — for instance, that many of their cybersecurity internship evaluations are done through Cyber Skyline. For anyone preparing to apply, that tip alone made the session worth it!
